Assumptions & the fine print

  • Toner. Genuine Xerox C7000 toner runs about 0.65¢/page black and 0.74¢/page per color at the ISO 5%-coverage standard. Marketing collateral covers far more than 5%, so the coverage presets scale that up: light ≈ $0.03, medium ≈ $0.07, heavy full-bleed ≈ $0.13 per side. Compatible (non-Xerox) toner can roughly halve these — drop the toner field if you plan to use it.
  • Maintenance. The drum is rated ~82,000 pages and the fuser ~100,000 pages; spread across pages that's roughly a penny-plus per side. Toggle it off to see consumables only.
  • Paper. Targets assume laser/digital-rated coated stock bought by the case. Inkjet-coated paper can damage a fuser — always buy laser-rated gloss.
  • UPS comparison. Built from published UPS Store color ranges ($0.59–$1.09/side) and typical cover-stock tiers. Your Brighton store (#2109) sets its own prices — drop in a real quote for an exact match.
  • Not included: the cutter/scorer labor (a few minutes per run), electricity (negligible), and your design time (the same whether you print in-house or out). Double-sided uses one sheet of paper but two sides of toner.
  • Reality check: in-house wins decisively on frequent short-to-mid runs. Keep UPS for true bulk (1,000s) and large-format, where their per-piece price and wide-format gear still beat you.
